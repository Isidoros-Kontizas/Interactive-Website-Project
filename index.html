Barcelona, Paris

About History

As the 21st century began, London embraced bold new landmarks to mark the new millennium. The Millennium Dome at Greenwich sparked debate, but other projects became instant icons. The London Eye, originally a temporary installation, quickly became a beloved fixture, offering breathtaking views to over four million visitors annually. Meanwhile, the British Museum’s Great Court received a stunning glass roof, transforming it into a grand, light-filled space. Thanks to these ambitious projects, London seamlessly blends its rich history with modern innovation.
